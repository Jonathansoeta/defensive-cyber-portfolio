# Privacy technique used online
A privacy technique I use online is **protecting my identity by using a fake name, a different random email address, and a fake age when I sign up to websites or apps**.

Instead of using my real name, my main email address, and my real age, I create:
- A **fake username**.
- A **separate email address** that is not connected to my personal accounts.
- A **fake age** that is close to my real age but not exact.

This makes it harder for strangers or attackers to link my online activity back to my real identity. If someone tries to search for my real name or email, they will not easily find my accounts or personal information.

This technique helps protect my privacy and reduces the risk of **doxxing**. I use it when I am testing websites, joining forums, or using apps where I do not need to share my true identity.

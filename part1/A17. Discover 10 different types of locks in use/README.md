# A17 – Discover 10 different types of locks in use

1. **Padlock**  
A removable lock with a shackle that can be attached to chains, lockers, or gates. It is commonly used for gates, storage units, and lockers.

2. **Deadbolt lock**  
A strong lock usually installed on external doors. It uses a solid metal bolt that extends into the door frame and is operated by a key or thumb turn.

3. **Knob lock**  
The lock is built into the door knob. It is often used on internal doors in houses or offices, but offers only basic security.

4. **Lever handle lock**  
A lock with a lever handle instead of a knob, common in offices and commercial buildings because it is easier to use.

5. **Mortise lock**  
A heavy-duty lock installed inside a pocket (mortise) in the door. It often combines a latch and a deadbolt and is used on many front doors and commercial doors.

6. **Rim lock / night latch**  
A lock mounted on the inside surface of the door, often with a latch that automatically locks when the door closes and can be opened with a key from outside.

7. **Smart lock**  
An electronic lock that can be controlled with a smartphone, PIN code, card, or fingerprint. Many smart locks use a traditional deadbolt mechanism with digital control.

8. **Cam lock**  
A small lock with a rotating metal piece (“cam”) on the back, commonly used on mailboxes, cabinets, and lockers.

9. **Combination lock**  
A lock opened by entering a number sequence instead of using a key, often used on safes, lockers, and some padlocks.

10. **Cylinder lock (Yale-type)**  
A common door lock that uses a key-operated cylinder with pin tumblers. Turning the key moves the internal mechanism to lock or unlock the door.
